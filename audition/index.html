Can reverberation break bi-stable interpretations of speech?

Crista Falk, Vivian Garcia, Somaia Saba

In this illusion, there are 4 different versions of the same audio. In the original, a phrase is being said by multiple people simultaneously; this sentence has several different stable interpretations including but not limited to: that isnâ€™t my receipt; lactates in pharmacy; baptism piracy; that is embarrassing. Each person is actually saying a different phrase, but in unison, it sounds like they are saying the same phrase, further ensuring ambiguity in the perception of the audio.

We aimed to clarify whether or not reverberation can break multistable interpretations of the audio. We added three different levels of reverberation to the audio; a small room condition, a large room condition, and a cathedral condition. Try listening to the audio with reverb and see if your interpretation changes.
